My name's Steve Rokitka (Rocketkar) - I'm a designer, writer and artist. I've been making things with words and images for most of my life, and doing it professionally for the better part of a decade. In that time I've designed a top-selling iOS app, created numerous websites and web assets, and designed enough t-shirts that they account for roughly half my wardrobe.

I spent three of the last four years as UI/UX lead at a startup, building a complex generative music app and all the assets, marketing materials, websites, and promo schwag that goes along with it. Once the app was released, I decided to take some time off and cross a few (okay, most) of the items off my bucket list. I painted a house, bought a motorcycle and learned to ride, took a Wilderness First Responder course, spent six months learning to fix cars at a community college, and continued my training in Krav Maga.

Taking a break from design was really incredible in that it helped deprogram me from a lot of the things that were holding me back professionally. From working with cars I learned to find something to measure to determine whether a job was successful. From training Krav Maga I learned how even a master continues to train and retrain their fundamentals. From getting first responder certfication I learned how a professional doesn't show up to a situation and take on that situation's energy - they show up and set the tone for how a problem is going to get solved.

Today, I'm back at work as a freelancer, and looking for awesome projets that I can be a part of. I've recently designed two novels, a corporate website, a nonprofit's brand identity, and in my opinion, I'm doing the best work of my life. If you've got something that needs solutions found, ideas given form, stories discovered or just a bit of snark interjected into a situation, drop me a line and let's get started.

Six Pieces From My Professional Life

  • Be someone people can look forward to being around.
  • What someone wants and what they say they want are often different. Good listening means reading between the lines.
  • Don't promise all the time. Just do what you say you're going to do, or don't say it.
  • People aren't using their logical brains as much as they think. They feel their way through most things. This is beautiful and terrifying and beautiful again.
  • Everyone really, really wants to feel listened to. You can disagree with someone and still make them feel listened to and treated with respect.
  • The way you do anything is the way you do everything. You can watch someone make a sandwich and it'll tell you everything, if you're paying attention.
